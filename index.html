Humsafar Trust

The Humsafar Trust founded in April 1994 by Ashok Row Kavi. It became the first openly Gay Community Based Organisation to be allotted space in a Municipal building in Mumbai and began its activities by inviting gay men and lesbian women to attend its workshops at its drop-in centre. The HIV interventions of The Humsafar Trust are supported by National AIDS Control Organization (NACO) and Mumbai District AIDS Control Society (MDACS) to provide health services through Public Health Care delivery systems and its In-house clinics.

Indian Aces

Indian Aces was started in 2014 by Dr Pragati Singh as a self/non-funded collective of, for, and by ace (asexual) and aspec (asexual spectrum) people from/in India. Over the years, they have expanded beyond asexuality to include hard-hitting research, advocacy, workshops and events across the subjects of gender, sexuality, diversity and LGBTQIA+. They serve as an online community platform, generating awareness as well as participating in various LGBTQIA+ events.

Sappho For Equality

Based in Kolkata, Sappho for Equality was established in October 2003 and is the only registered organization in Eastern India that works for the rights and social justice of sexually marginalized women and transmen (female to male transpersons). Their vision is to see a society free of sexuality-based discrimination, where rights of the sexual minorities will be ensured. Sappho for Equality works with a three-pronged approach—community empowerment, addressing general civil society and networking with other groups/organisations and advocacy/lobbying with the State.

Gauri Sawant
Gauri rose to prominence after appearing in a Vicks advertising, but she has been an activist for transgender rights for much longer. In fact, she was the first transgender person to petition the Supreme Court of India for transgender adoption rights in 2014.

Seth, the son of Prem Seth and Leila Seth, the first woman Chief Justice of a High Court in India, attended some of the country’s top schools before moving to England for further education. He is 61-year-old Padma Shri winner. sing his sadness at the recent ruling criminalising gay intercourse.
Sonal Giani
Sonal Giani is a well-known LGBTQ activist and actor who is well-known for her pioneering work in addressing lesbian, bisexual, and transgender women’s concerns, as well as LGBTQ youth work. She had also co-founded one of India’s largest LGBTQ youth programmes, Yaariyan, as well as Umang, a Mumbai-based lesbian-bisexual-transgender organisation.
